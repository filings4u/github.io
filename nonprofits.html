Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a Nonprofit and 501(c)(3) status?
"Nonprofit" is a state-level legal status for your corporation. "501(c)(3)" is a federal tax-exempt status granted by the IRS. You must first form your nonprofit corporation at the state level before you can apply for federal tax exemption.
How many board members do I need?
Most states require a minimum of three directors. For 501(c)(3) purposes, the IRS generally prefers at least three individuals who are not related to each other to ensure proper oversight and public accountability.
What exactly is a Registered Agent?
A Registered Agent is a person or entity designated to receive official legal documents and government correspondence on behalf of your Nonprofit. Every corporation is legally required by the state to maintain one to ensure "Good Standing."
Does the $100 fee include the IRS application fee?
No. Our $100 service fee covers the professional preparation and filing of your state articles. The IRS charges a separate user fee ($275 for Form 1023-EZ or $600 for the full Form 1023) which is paid directly to the Department of Treasury.
Can I pay myself a salary from a Nonprofit?
Yes. Nonprofit employees and officers can be paid "reasonable compensation" for the services they provide. This must be documented and approved by the board to ensure it meets IRS guidelines against "private inurement."
How long does the entire process take?
State incorporation typically takes 1–3 business days. Once incorporated, obtaining an EIN is instant. The IRS tax-exempt approval can take anywhere from 4 weeks (for the 1023-EZ) to 6 months (for the full 1023).
What are Bylaws and why do I need them?
Bylaws are the internal "rulebook" for your organization. They dictate how directors are elected, how meetings are conducted, and how the nonprofit is governed. The IRS requires these to be adopted to grant tax-exempt status.
Will a professional agent help me stay compliant?
Yes. We track your state’s Annual Report deadlines and 990-N filing dates. We send automated reminders well in advance so your nonprofit never accidentally loses its tax-exempt status.
