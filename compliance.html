Keep Your Company Compliant

Forming your company is just the first step. Avoid penalties, dissolution, and state fines by staying on top of corporate compliance obligations.

1. Annual Reports & Statements

Most states require LLCs and Corporations to file periodic informational updates—either annually or biennially. Missing these filings can cause your business status to fall into "Not In Good Standing," leading to late fees or eventual administrative dissolution by the state.

2. Franchise Taxes

Certain states (like Delaware and California) require business entities to pay an annual privilege or franchise tax to operate. This fee is mandatory regardless of whether your business generated revenue during the year.

3. Maintaining a Registered Agent

Every business must continuously maintain a registered physical address in its state of formation to accept formal legal notices or service of process. Using our ongoing Registered Agent service ensures you never miss a time-sensitive lawsuit or state notice.
