Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is a Registered Agent?
A Registered Agent is a person or entity designated to receive official legal documents and government correspondence on behalf of your LLC. This includes Service of Process (lawsuit notifications), tax notices, and annual report reminders. Every LLC is legally required by the state to maintain one.
Can I be my own Registered Agent?
Technically, yes. Most states allow you to act as your own agent if you have a physical address in the state where you formed your LLC. However, this requires your personal address to be listed on public record, and you must be available at that address during all standard business hours.
Why should I use a professional service instead of my home address?
The primary reason is Privacy. When you use a professional service like ours, our commercial address goes on the public record, not your home address. This stops solicitors, junk mailers, and even disgruntled customers from finding out where you live.
What happens if I don't have a Registered Agent?
Failure to maintain an active Registered Agent can result in your business losing its "Good Standing" status with the state. This can lead to hefty fines, the inability to enter into legal contracts, and eventually, the administrative dissolution (shutdown) of your LLC.
Do I need a Registered Agent in every state?
You need a Registered Agent in the state where you formed your LLC (your "domestic" state). If you register your business to do work in other states (Foreign Qualification), you will also need a Registered Agent with a physical address in each of those states.
How do I receive my documents?
We provide instant digital delivery. The moment we receive a legal document or official notice at our office, we scan it and upload it to your secure dashboard. You will receive an immediate email notification so you never miss a critical deadline.
Will a professional agent help me stay compliant?
Yes. Part of our service includes tracking your state’s Annual Report deadlines. We send you automated reminders well in advance of the due date so you can avoid late fees and keep your business compliant with the Secretary of State.
Can a Registered Agent be a P.O. Box?
No. State law requires a Registered Agent to have a physical street address where a person can be physically served with legal papers. P.O. Boxes and private mailbox rentals are not permitted for this role.
What happens if my business is sued?
If your business is served with a lawsuit, the process server will deliver the papers to our office. We accept them discreetly and notify you immediately. This prevents the embarrassment of being served in front of customers, employees, or family members at your place of business or home.
How do I switch my Registered Agent to you?
The process is simple. When you sign up for our service, we help you file a "Change of Agent" form with the Secretary of State. Once the state processes the filing, we officially become your shield for privacy and compliance.
