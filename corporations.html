Corporate Intelligence

What is the difference between a C-Corp and an S-Corp?
A C-Corp is a separate taxable entity. An S-Corp is a tax designation for corporations that meet certain IRS requirements, allowing profits to "pass through" to owners to avoid double taxation.
Do I need Bylaws?
Yes. Bylaws are legally required for corporations. They outline how the company is governed, how meetings are held, and the powers of directors/officers.
Can an LLC become a Corporation?
Yes. This is called a statutory conversion. However, starting as the correct entity from day one saves significant legal and filing fees.
