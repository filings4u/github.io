Why Register a DBA?

A "Doing Business As" (DBA) name—also known as a fictitious name—allows you to operate multiple brands under one legal entity.

Brand Diversification

Launch new product lines or services under unique names while keeping all legal and tax reporting under your existing LLC or Corporation.

Banking & Payments

Open a business bank account and accept checks or credit card payments made out to your new brand name instead of your personal name.

Public Credibility

Establish a professional presence in your market. Use your DBA on websites, business cards, and marketing materials with legal authority.

DBA Intelligence

Does a DBA provide liability protection?
No. A DBA is just a name. Liability protection comes from your underlying business structure, like an LLC or Corporation.
Do I need a separate tax ID for a DBA?
No. Your DBA operates under the EIN (Tax ID) of your existing legal entity or your SSN if you are a sole proprietor.
What is "Publication" in DBA filings?
Some states require you to announce your new DBA name in a local newspaper. We handle the coordination of these legal notices for you in our Professional tier.
